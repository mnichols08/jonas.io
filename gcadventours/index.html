Discover what's in our backyard

Take a look at what surrounds you.

You just might fall in love with this place.

Garrett County is simply one of the most photogenic places in all of Appalachia. It hosts miles of hiking and biking trails scattered throughout the county. It's highest peak offers sights into West Virginia as far as Mount Storm. A dam which finished construction in 1925 created a lake which covers 399 acres of surface area! Some refer to Deep Creek Lake as Lake Tahoe of the East. With 65 miles of shoreline surrounded by often snow covered mountains, one could guess why!

There is even more to it than the eye beholds.

The weather and climate make this area unique for each visit. Everybody knows that Maryland has some funky weather, so naturally Garrett County is no exception for that rule. You better pack for snow even in July. But seriously, the weather attracts different folk for different reason. While some look for snow in the forecast to come up for a big ski weekend, others watch the rain forecast and prepare for Class V whitewater. Whether you are into fishing, riding ATVs, camping, mountain biking, bouldering, and so much more...This is just one adventure of a place.
